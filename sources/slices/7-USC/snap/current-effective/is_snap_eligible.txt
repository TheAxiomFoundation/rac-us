Federal SNAP current-effective household eligibility composite

This source slice combines the operative federal eligibility elements reflected in
current SNAP household eligibility determinations.

7 USC 2017(a): State agencies may issue SNAP allotments only to households
certified as eligible to participate in the supplemental nutrition assistance
program.

7 USC 2014(a): Households in which each member receives supplemental security
income or benefits under a State program funded under part A of title IV of the
Social Security Act shall be eligible to participate in SNAP, and otherwise
household income and financial resources shall be limited.

7 USC 2014(c): A household shall be ineligible if its monthly net income exceeds
the applicable poverty line and, unless the household contains an elderly or
disabled member, if its monthly gross income exceeds 130 percent of the poverty
line.

7 USC 2014(g)(1): A household otherwise eligible to participate in SNAP shall
not be eligible if its financial resources exceed the applicable resource limit.

7 USC 2015(d)(1)(A): A household member subject to SNAP work requirements is not
eligible if the member refuses or fails to comply with those work requirements.

7 USC 2015(e): A person enrolled at least half-time in an institution of higher
education is not eligible unless a statutory student exception applies.

7 USC 2015(f): No person otherwise eligible as a household member may
participate in SNAP unless the person is a resident of the United States and a
citizen, national, or other qualifying eligible noncitizen.
